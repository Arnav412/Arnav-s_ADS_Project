Frequently Asked Questions

What is post‚Äësurgical brain tumor detection?

After surgery, doctors keep checking to see if any tumor is left or has come back. They look at scans and how you feel to decide the next steps.

An early scan gives a starting point. Later scans are compared to it so changes are easier to understand.

How soon after surgery is imaging performed?

The first MRI usually happens within 1‚Äì3 days after surgery. This helps reduce confusion from normal healing changes.

Future scans are planned for you. The timing depends on your tumor type and how you are doing.

Which scans are commonly used?

Most people get an MRI with contrast. Sometimes extra scan types are used to give more detail when the picture is not clear.

These extra scans can show how much blood flow there is, how dense the tissue is, or other simple signals that help doctors tell healing from tumor.

What symptoms should prompt earlier review?

Call your care team sooner if you have new or worse headaches, seizures, weakness, numbness, vision changes, or trouble speaking or thinking.

If symptoms change quickly or keep getting worse, do not wait. Ask for advice.

Is this information medical advice?

No. This page is for general information only. Your doctors know your case and can give you the right advice.
